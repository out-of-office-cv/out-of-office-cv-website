# Impeccable

## Design Context

### Users

Two distinct audiences, both reading in a considered rather than urgent mode:

- **Readers (primary)**: Australian citizens, journalists, researchers, and civically-engaged members of the public. Context: desktop or phone, likely triggered by a news story ("what's ex-MP X doing now?") or by curiosity. They want to see the record, trust it, and follow sources.
- **Contributors (secondary)**: a smaller set of people who add or verify gig entries via the GitHub-backed contribute flow. More patient, more technical, but not developers first --- they're here for the civic purpose.

Job to be done: *"Show me, clearly and without spin, what this person has done since leaving parliament, and where that information came from."*

### Brand Personality

Three words: **archival, editorial, unshowy.**

- Voice: plain, factual, sourced. Reads like a reference work, not a campaign.
- Tone: confident about the data, quiet about itself. Doesn't editorialise, doesn't moralise --- lets the list of gigs do the arguing.
- Emotional goals: **trust, clarity, legitimacy.** A reader should feel they've landed on something maintained by people who care about getting it right.
- Explicitly NOT: a dashboard, a campaign site, a SaaS product, a data-viz showpiece, or anything that reads as politically aligned.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Editorial / print-inspired civic record.**

Think a well-typeset reference publication or a serious public-interest investigative site --- strong typographic hierarchy, generous whitespace, distinctive display face paired with a refined reading face, considered use of rules and small metadata type. Columnar where it makes sense. The data itself is the decoration.

- **Theme**: light mode only (decided during the Astro 6 port). A light, slightly warm paper-like background suits the archival/editorial framing; avoid bright pure white.
- **Palette**: open to replacing the current `#5672cd` brand blue. Must avoid red/blue partisan coding. Prefer a single restrained accent (e.g. a muted ink, a deep bottle green, an oxblood, a desaturated indigo) plus carefully tinted neutrals. The chamber and party badges are functional data colours and should stay visually subordinate to the overall palette --- treat them as small inline markers, not brand colours.
- **Typography**: pair a distinctive (non-reflex) display face with a refined body face. Do not ship system-ui as the final choice. Avoid the obvious "AI editorial" fonts (Fraunces, Playfair, Cormorant, Instrument Serif, DM Serif, etc.) --- look wider when the time comes.
- **Anti-references**: corporate SaaS dashboards, hero-metric landing pages, glassmorphism, cyan-on-dark "data platform" aesthetics, political campaign sites, identical card grids.

### Constraints

- Static Astro 6 site, deployed to GitHub Pages. Server-rendered Astro components by default; Svelte 5 islands only where interactivity is needed (search, contribute form, draft gigs).
- Light mode only. No dark-mode toggle.
- Australian English throughout.
- Build-time data (CSV + JSON); no runtime API.

### Accessibility

- **WCAG 2.2 AA** as the floor: contrast, focus visibility, keyboard-complete operation, no motion-only affordances.
- Honour `prefers-reduced-motion` on every transition.
- Semantic HTML first; ARIA only where semantics run out.

### Design Principles

1. **The record speaks.** The data --- pollies, gigs, sources, dates --- is the hero. Chrome, decoration, and UI flourishes should recede so the content reads cleanly.
2. **Editorial hierarchy, not dashboard hierarchy.** Lean on type scale, weight, rules, and whitespace to organise the page --- not on boxed cards, big numbers, or accent colour. Left-aligned, asymmetric, print-literate.
3. **Quiet confidence, no partisanship.** Restrained palette, neutral voice, visible sources. Nothing that reads as taking a side or marketing itself.
4. **Show the provenance.** Sources, verification status, and "last updated" should be first-class visual citizens --- not footnotes. Trust is built by making the workings visible.
5. **Civic, not corporate.** When a choice could go SaaS-dashboard or public-record, go public-record. This site should feel closer to a library catalogue than to a product.
